Zyappy — Roles, Access & Permissions
RBAC — Product Summary
The system defines who can access what and where using:
Roles (user type)
Permissions (allowed actions)
Modules (feature access)
Store scope (data visibility)
Key Highlights:
7 roles are defined from platform-level to store-level users.
87 granular permissions allow flexible role creation.
Access is enforced at multiple levels (routes, modules, data scope).
Store-based filtering ensures users only see relevant business data.
Special capabilities like impersonation enable debugging and support.
Executive Summary
Zyappy employs a robust 7-role hierarchy to manage access and permissions,
complemented by 87 granular permissions distributed across 12 operational
categories. Access control is implemented at four distinct layers: session
authentication, layout guards, route filtering, and inline page checks. Additionally, the
KDS module operates a parallel permission subsystem with 11 dedicated permission
keys, ensuring comprehensive security and operational segregation.
1. Role Definitions
This section outlines the seven distinct roles within the Zyappy platform, detailing
their internal keys, scope, purpose, landing pages, and specific capabilities and
limitations.
1.1 Platform Super Admin
Property Detail
Internal Key PLATFORM_SUPER_ADMIN
Scope Global — across all brands and stores
Purpose
Platform operator responsible for onboarding, brand provisioning, and
system health.
Landing
Page
/platform/brands
Capabilities:
Access the Platform Brands dashboard to view, create, and manage all brands.
Impersonate any brand (2-hour session) to diagnose issues within their
backoffice.
Full read/write access to every backoffice and KDS route when impersonating.
All 87 permissions are granted by default.
Limitations:
N/A — this role possesses the highest privilege level (“God mode”).
Special Behavior:
When navigating /platform/* , the sidebar exclusively displays Brands.
When impersonating at /backoffice/* , the sidebar shows a scoped setup view:
Items, Integrations, KDS Master, KDS Expo.
An Impersonation Banner is prominently displayed at the top of the page during
active sessions.
Impersonation sessions automatically expire after 2 hours.
1.2 Brand Admin
Property Detail
Internal Key BRAND_ADMIN
Scope Brand-wide — all stores under one brand
Purpose The brand owner or franchise operator with full control over their business.
Landing Page /backoffice/home
Capabilities:
Full read/write access to all 14 backoffice routes.
Access to KDS Master & Expo screens.
Management of all stores without restriction ( getManagedStoreIds() → 'all' ).
Creation, editing, and deletion of custom roles, and assignment to staff.
Creation and management of email campaigns and audience segments.
Viewing of AI call analytics dashboard and detail pages.
Configuration of integrations, business settings, and inventory.
Management of the Web Shop.
Viewing and exporting of financial reports (payments, refunds, payouts).
Access to the “New Sale” button in the sidebar (POS entry point).
Limitations:
Cannot access /platform/* routes (exclusive to Platform Super Admin).
Cannot impersonate other brands.
Permissions: All 87 granted (system role, non-editable).

6. Special Features by Role
6.1 Impersonation (Super Admin Only)
Property Value
Who can initiate Platform Super Admin only
Duration 2 hours (auto-expiry)
Visual indicator Yellow warning banner at top of backoffice
Audit trail IMPERSONATION_STARTED and IMPERSONATION_ENDED events logged
Access granted Full read/write to the impersonated brand’s backoffice
6.2 “New Sale” Button (Sidebar)
Visible to: Brand Admin, Admin, Store Manager only. Hidden for: Platform Super
Admin (when not impersonating), Employee, POS User, KDS User.
6.3 Email Campaign Segments
Only Platform Super Admin, Brand Admin, and Admin can create, edit, duplicate,
toggle, or delete audience segments. Store Managers and below either see segments
as read-only or have no access to the module.
6.4 KDS Station Mode
For shared kitchen screens, a device can be paired to a station by a Manager/Admin.
Once paired:
Viewing orders works with the station token alone (no individual login required).
Sensitive actions (cancel, delay, re-fire) require a 4-digit PIN re-authentication
that creates a 10-minute action session.
The audit log records both the station identity and the individual user who
entered the PIN.
